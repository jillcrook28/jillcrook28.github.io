Thailand is known for being humid and having hot summers. You'll want to pack clothes that will be comfortable for long days of traveling, walking, and being outside in the sun! 

Remember to pack light, as we'll be hopping around the country and will be hauling our bags on trains and planes. It will be easier to do laundry than to pack lots and carry the heavy suitcase. 

Bring clothes that you don't mind sweating in or getting wet. Shorts, t-shirts, tank tops, and one modest outfit for entering temples that require dress code. And definitely bring a swimsuit!

When in Thailand, we'll dive into the incredible world of Thai cuisine! However, discretion is advised with street food. Bring tums or special medicine if you have a sensitive stomach. 

Dylan says, "Drinking Coke helps, too."

See some famous Thai dishes!

Thailand is on ICT time (Indochina Time), meaning it is 15 hours ahead of California time. Keep that in mind as you contact your friends and family back at home, because it could be afternoon in Thailand, but the middle of the night back in the states!

Things to do

Here are some of the things we can do at the locations we're visiting!

Doi Suthep

We can visit Doi Suthep in Chiang Mai, one of the most sacred pilgrimage spots in Thailand! The village features golden buildings, a staircase covered with stunning dragon scales, and overall amazing views of the city!

The Grand Palace

Located in Bangkok, the Grand Palace is a beautiful royal villa that was home to Thai kings until 1932. There is a dress code- men must wear long pants, women must cover legs to above the knee, and no tight or shoulder-revealing clothing. There is also an entrance fee of 500 baht.

Chai Lai Orchid

Embark on a captivating journey to Thailand's breathtaking Chai Lai Orchid, where lush jungles, majestic elephants, and authentic Thai hospitality await, offering our family an enchanting escape filled with cherished moments and unforgettable adventures in nature's embrace. Immerse yourselves in a harmonious blend of beauty and cultural immersion, creating memories that will forever linger in our hearts.

Resort Style!

Our final few days in Thailand, we will be able to enjoy beach time at Koh Samui! Staying at the Chaweng Noi Pool Villa, we will finally be able to relax by the water. If we feel adventerous, there is plenty to see and do at Koh Samui besides relax- we could hike to waterfalls, visit a monk cave, and explore Fisherman's village; a walking street market!
